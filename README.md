# SNHU CS-330: Computational Graphics and Visualization

This repository contains projects and assignments completed for CS-330.

## Topics Covered
- OpenGL rendering
- 3D transformations
- Lighting and shading
- Camera controls

## Final Project Reflection

### 1. How do I approach designing software?

This project has helped me better understand how to break down a complex idea into smaller, manageable parts. Instead of trying to build the entire 3D scene at once, I focused on individual components like shapes, lighting, and textures, then combined them step by step. This approach made the process much more manageable and helped reduce errors during the learning process.

Throughout the project, I followed a structured design process by starting with a general idea of the scene and then refining it as I went. I also learned the importance of planning object placement, lighting, and materials early on since those decisions impact the overall look and feel of the final scene. In future work, I can apply this same strategy of breaking problems into smaller pieces and building incrementally.

### 2. How do I approach developing programs?

During development, I relied heavily on trial and error and iterative testing. I would make small changes, run the program, and then adjust based on what I observed. This helped me quickly identify issues and better understand how different parts of the code affected the final output.

One important strategy I developed was debugging more effectively, especially when working with shaders and rendering. I learned that even small mismatches between the CPU and GPU code can cause major issues, so attention to detail is critical. Over time, my approach evolved from guessing and checking to being more intentional with changes and testing one thing at a time. This made the development process more efficient and less frustrating.

### 3. How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

This project gave me a better understanding of computational graphics and how 3D visualization works, which is useful not only in game development but also in areas like simulation and data visualization. It showed me how important it is to present information visually in a clear and effective way.

In terms of my future goals, the skills I developed, such as problem-solving, debugging, and breaking down complex systems, are directly applicable to software engineering as a whole. Even if I do not specialize in graphics, the concepts I learned in this course will help me build more efficient and well-structured programs in the future.
